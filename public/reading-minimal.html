B. Cities such as Singapore and Copenhagen have pioneered the "biophilic city" model, integrating green corridors connecting parks and waterways. The Value of Urban Green Spaces
Adapted from Cambridge IELTS Academic

A. Urban green spaces are increasingly recognised not merely as pleasant amenities but as critical infrastructure. Research across dozens of cities demonstrates that access to nature is associated with measurable improvements in mental and physical health outcomes for residents of all socioeconomic backgrounds.

B. Cities such as Singapore and Copenhagen have pioneered the "biophilic city" model, integrating green corridors connecting parks and waterways. Studies tracking residents over five years found 23% lower rates of anxiety disorders among those with consistent green access.

C. A University of Exeter study found that property values within 500 metres of a park were on average 8% higher than comparable properties elsewhere. Cities with established green networks also attracted a greater proportion of skilled workers.

D. Critics point to green gentrification — improvements raise property values and can displace the communities meant to benefit. Planners face the challenge of balancing ecological investment with equitable access.
