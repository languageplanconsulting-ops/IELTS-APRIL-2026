The Dawn of Tea

ATea is the most widely consumed beverage in the world after water, yet its origins are wrapped in legend. According to Chinese tradition, the emperor Shen Nung discovered tea in 2737 BC when leaves from a wild tree blew into his pot of boiling water entirely by accident.

BFor centuries tea remained a closely guarded Chinese secret. It was not until the ninth century that a Japanese monk, returning from study abroad, carried seeds home and began the first cultivation outside China.

CTea reached Europe through Portuguese and Dutch traders in the early seventeenth century. The British, later the world's most devoted tea drinkers, were comparatively late to adopt the habit.
